Transparent pricing information for your siding project

Understanding Siding Costs

The cost of a siding project depends on several key factors, including the material you choose, the size and complexity of your home, and any additional services required. While every project is unique, we believe in providing transparent pricing information to help you plan your budget.

Key Factors That Affect Siding Costs:

  • {% include icons/material.html %}

    Siding Material

    Vinyl siding is typically the most affordable option, while wood and fiber cement fall into the mid-range, and natural stone or brick veneer represent premium choices.

  • {% include icons/home.html %}

    Home Size and Layout

    Larger homes require more materials and labor. Complex layouts with multiple stories, unusual angles, or hard-to-reach areas can increase installation time and cost.

  • {% include icons/remove.html %}

    Removal of Existing Siding

    If old siding needs to be removed, this adds to the overall project cost. The type and condition of existing siding affects removal costs.

  • {% include icons/repair.html %}

    Repairs and Preparation

    Any necessary repairs to the underlying structure, such as replacing rotted wood or addressing moisture issues, will add to the total cost.

  • {% include icons/insulation.html %}

    Insulation

    Adding or upgrading insulation during your siding project improves energy efficiency but increases material and labor costs.
